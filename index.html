Climate Quick Facts

The average carbon footprint per person in India is about 1,900 kg (1.9 tonnes) of CO2e per year, which is lower than the global average of 4,000 kg. However, urban Indian lifestyles often exceed 4,000 kg. To avoid a 2°C rise in global temperatures, the personal footprint needs to drop under 2,000 kg.

Step 3: Diet & Food Habits

Dietary Habits

Select the choice that best matches your typical diet.

Why does diet matter? Food production accounts for nearly 26% of global emissions. Beef generates approximately 60 kg of CO2e per kg of meat, while plant-based foods like peas generate only 0.9 kg. Shifting to a vegetarian or low-meat diet is one of the single most effective individual climate actions.
